Key Concepts

  • Sculpt mode brush tools: draw brush raises along normals, grab brush moves freely, shift for smooth
  • Voxel remesh: press R to set grid size, Ctrl+R to execute; creates even face density across surface
  • Dynamic topology (Dyntopo) adds faces as you sculpt but creates uneven density; voxel remesh is preferable
  • Switching from OpenGL to Vulkan in Blender preferences dramatically improves sculpt performance
  • High poly sculpt does not need UVs; only the low poly retopologized mesh needs UVs for baking
  • Retopology: drawing new quad-based geometry on top of the high poly sculpt surface
  • PolyQuilt add-on and Key Ops Toolkit streamline manual retopology with direct face drawing tools
  • Baking transfers sculpt detail from high poly geometry into normal map pixels on the low poly UV

Key Concepts

  • Smart masks require baked mesh maps; without baking, only the grunge component works, not the cavity or edge component
  • Apply subdivision surface at one level lower for UV unwrapping; subdivide after the texture round-trip if needed
  • UV Unwrap options: Minimum Stretch (recommended), Angle Based, Conformal are all similar; Project From View ignores seams
  • Baking high poly to low poly in Substance Painter: bake mesh maps including normal, AO, curvature, thickness
  • After baking, smart masks and filters use sculpt-quality mesh map data on the low poly UV
  • Normal map strength can be dialed down in Blender material nodes if height detail is too extreme
  • HDRI setup in Blender: switch Shading panel to World, add Environment Texture node, load equirectangular HDR image
  • EEVEE ray tracing fills in shadows and light bounces for a more natural render at lower computational cost than Cycles
  • Subsurface scattering for candle wax: requires a point light nearby to see the effect; wax radius value controls scatter depth
  • Week 12 project: sculpt candle, retopologize, bake in Substance Painter, render final table scene with HDRI and SSS

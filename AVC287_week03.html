Week Overview

This week dives deep into two fundamental animation principles: anticipation and squash & stretch. Monday covers anticipation fundamentals with gradations from subtle to extreme, multi-axis anticipation with rotation and position, hammer swing and pendulum physics, then transitions into squash and stretch with volume preservation, bounce animation, and jiggle techniques. Wednesday features extensive student feedback on hammer projects followed by quad topology sphere creation, invisible box rigging, bouncing ball blockout with rolling animation, and applied squash and stretch with advanced timing.

Key Concepts

  • Anticipation prepares the viewer for the main action (windup before throw)
  • Gradations range from subtle realistic to extreme cartoon anticipation
  • Origin point placement determines how objects rotate and deform
  • Pendulum physics demonstrates natural swing timing and momentum
  • Volume preservation: squash wider = stretch taller (constant volume)
  • Jiggle adds secondary motion that follows the primary animation

Key Concepts

  • Quad topology spheres deform cleanly for squash and stretch animation
  • Invisible box rigging separates position, rotation, and scale controls
  • Parent-child hierarchy lets you animate different properties independently
  • Blockout animation establishes major poses before refining details
  • Rolling requires syncing rotation speed with translation distance
  • Ground contact frames need extra attention for believable bouncing

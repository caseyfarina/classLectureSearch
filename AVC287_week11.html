Key Concepts

  • Euler rotation order determines the final position of an object depending on axis priority.
  • Quaternions avoid gimbal lock but are harder to read in the Graph Editor than Euler rotations.
  • Blocking a run cycle requires distinct contact, passing, and airborne 'hang' moments.
  • Nudging or offsetting keyframes creates 'causality' and prevents the animation from looking stiff.
  • Velocity handles in the Graph Editor can be stretched to emphasize fast foot movement and leaping.
  • Unparenting knee and elbow poles can simplify tracking during complex movements.
  • Motion blur settings like shutter speed and steps are critical for communicating high-speed motion.

Key Concepts

  • Wide-angle lenses exaggerate form while telephoto lenses compress space toward an orthographic view.
  • Silhouette readability depends on both body positioning and effective camera framing.
  • The center of mass must be balanced over the feet to ensure a plausible standing or moving pose.
  • Walk cycles require proper hip rotation and subtle timing offsets between limbs to feel natural.
  • Use Control+B in the Blender timeline to bind cameras to markers for automatic switching between shots.
  • Animation reels should be presented with consistent framing, helpful text labels, and appropriate lighting.
  • Avoid locking out knees in IK chains by adjusting the vertical distance between the hips and feet.
  • Loop phasing allows you to offset cycles of different body parts to create more fluid motion.

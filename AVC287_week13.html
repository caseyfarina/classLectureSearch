Key Concepts

  • UV coordinates are normalized values between 0 and 1 on a Cartesian plane.
  • Sprite sheets improve performance by loading multiple animation frames as a single image memory.
  • Multiple UV maps allow independent control over different facial features like eyes and mouth.
  • The Snap math node quantizes values to prevent interpolation between animation frames.
  • Drivers link physical object movement (like a controller cube) to shader parameters.
  • Map Range nodes translate real-world controller coordinates into specific UV positions.
  • Emission shaders create a flat, '2D' aesthetic for stylized 3D characters.

Key Concepts

  • Use 'Stretch To' and 'Damp Track' constraints to create complex rigging for flexible objects.
  • Maintain the center of mass over the feet to ensure character balance during jumps.
  • Distinguish styles by using motion blur for realism and smears/squash for cartoons.
  • Apply Grease Pencil line art modifiers to entire collections for a consistent comic look.
  • Simulate gravity by decelerating on the ascent and accelerating on the descent.
  • Leverage 'coyote time' and exaggerated anticipation to enhance cartoon timing.
  • Incorporate Foley and royalty-free music in After Effects rather than Blender.
  • Review silhouettes to ensure poses are readable even without textures.

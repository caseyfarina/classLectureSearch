Key Concepts

  • WAV files are preferred for Blender import to reduce processing overhead during playback.
  • Separate UV maps allow for independent control of eyes, head, and mouth textures.
  • Constant interpolation is essential for snappy, sprite-based mouth shape changes.
  • Minimum of two frames per mouth shape prevents visual flickering at 24fps.
  • The decimate tool helps clean up dense motion capture data for easier manual editing.
  • The Play Blast technique provides a fast way to render previews with synchronized audio.

Key Concepts

  • Always use local space for drivers to ensure facial rigs remain functional when the character moves.
  • Sound design is a critical post-processing step that significantly increases the perceived quality of an animation.
  • Differentiate realistic and cartoon versions through timing, exaggerated arcs, and distinct camera angles.
  • In run cycles, maintain the hips forward relative to the feet to suggest proper center of mass and momentum.
  • Screen space line thickness in Grease Pencil creates a more consistent 'hand-drawn' look regardless of camera zoom.
  • Syncing audio waveforms directly in the Blender graph editor is essential for accurate lip-syncing and impact timing.
  • Avoid 'linear' camera moves; use cuts between shots or continuous cinematic motion for a more professional feel.
